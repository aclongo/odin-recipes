Mediterranean Chickpeas and Rice

This is one of my favorite "staple" recipes. The ingredients last for a long time and tend to be things you already have on hand (especially if you do ground parmesan and dried parsley). It's easy to put together and absolutely delicious. I would recommend measuring out the basil, oregano, salt and pepper ahead of time into a small bowl while the chickpeas are cooking in step 3.

I tweaked this recipe by adding the kalamata olives and the option to replace the parmesan with feta for more of a Mediterranean feel, if you have them on hand!

Recipe by Lisa.Conklin on Food.com

Ingredients

  • 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/3 cup chopped parsley
  • 2 tablespoons sun-dried tomatoes packed in oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped kalamata olives
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1/3 cup parmesan (or less feta cheese)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup basmati rice (uncooked)
  • 2 cups water

Steps

  1. Cook water, rice, thyme, and bay leaf and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil and half of the butter in a skillet and then add chickpeas.
  3. When chickpeas start to lightly brown (about 5 mins) add parsley and the sun-dried tomatoes and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Add oregano, basil, salt, and pepper and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and mix rice and chickpeas together until rice is a golden color.
  6. Mix in remaining butter (cubed) and Parmesan cheese.
